Citizens and civic bodies, fixing together.

A shared platform to report civic issues, bring together everyone affected, and resolve them — through community action, civic partnership, or both.

The challenge

Civic needs are growing faster than civic capacity

Scattered information

Reports arrive through helplines, apps, social media and local offices — no single, shared picture of what needs attention where.

Unclear ownership

It's often unclear which department or office handles a given issue, so requests move between desks and lose momentum.

Untapped goodwill

Residents who report rarely hear back — while local knowledge, volunteer hours and willing partners go unused.

Small problems become expensive ones — and residents and civic teams alike are stretched thinner.

The idea

Every issue has a community. fix brings it together.

Gather the affected

One report becomes a shared effort: everyone who lives with the broken road, dark street or flooded junction finds each other on the issue page.

Decide together

The community discusses countermeasures, votes on what would work best, and agrees what to prioritize — guided by local knowledge.

Act, together

The community contributes directly, works with the office responsible, or both — with clear, shared visibility for everyone involved.

How issues get resolved

Two paths to fixed

Path one

Community action

  • Volunteer drives with skill-tagged sign-ups
  • Funds and materials pledged against a published list of needs
  • Fix-it days with map meeting points and attendance
  • Before/after photos document every completed effort
Path two

Civic partnership

  • Each issue is routed to the department and role that handles it
  • Meetings, requests and updates — kept in one shared record
  • Commitments with timelines: completed, on track, or needs attention
  • Community and authority work side by side until it's done

Most issues take both: the community starts, the civic body finishes — or the other way around.

The platform

One platform, four pillars

Report

Geo-tagged issues with photos, category and severity — potholes, signals, footpaths, streetlights, drainage.

Deliberate

Open discussion and proposed countermeasures, voted on with reasons by the residents affected.

Prioritize

A transparent priority score — votes, severity, age, people affected — and a live locality dashboard.

Mobilize

Volunteers, pledges, adopted streets and coordination with civic offices — turning agreed priorities into action.

The backbone

Shared accountability, three ways

Civic bodies

Commitments and timelines are visible on each issue — and completed work earns public recognition, issue by issue.

Citizens

Reports are verified by neighbours, votes are on record, and reputation grows with verified reports and kept pledges.

Communities

Mobilization plans, pledges and fix-it events are tracked to completion — follow-through is visible, not just intent.

A shared scoreboard built on partnership and transparency — everyone's contribution counts, and everyone's contribution shows.

Know who can help

From your ward to the Chief Minister

Every issue is matched to the people responsible for that location — across both the administrative chain and the elected representatives — so the community always knows who to approach next.

Administrative chain
  • Ward Engineer
  • Executive Engineer
  • Zonal Commissioner
  • Municipal Commissioner
  • State Department → Chief Minister
Elected representatives
  • Corporator / Ward Member
  • MLA — constituency
  • MP — Lok Sabha constituency
  • State Minister
  • Chief Minister

Hierarchies adapt to each state and to rural three-tier panchayati raj. A respectful escalation ladder keeps the right people informed when issues stay open.

Corporates & institutions

Adopt a street

A street, junction or park — adopted

Companies, institutions and associations commit CSR funds, materials and employee volunteer hours to a defined stretch for a defined period.

CSR that's visible and verifiable

Every contribution — funds, hours, repairs — is logged against real issues with before/after evidence, and exported as a compliance-ready impact report.

Employees on the ground

Team volunteering days plug straight into community fix-it events — corporates work alongside residents and civic staff.

Recognition the whole neighbourhood sees

Adopted streets carry a tasteful "Adopted by" badge on the map and on the ground — lasting civic goodwill for the adopter.

The proposal

Pilot: three localities, six months

Months 1–2

Launch

Deploy in 3 pilot localities of a willing city, town or panchayat. Onboard community groups, NGOs, a corporate adopter and local officials. Seed and verify the first issues.

Months 3–4

Resolve

Run weekly prioritization with local committees. Deliver the first community fix-it days, an adopted-street program, and civic-led repairs from the shared queue.

Months 5–6

Evaluate

Measure resolution rate, time-to-fix, commitments met, participation and satisfaction against baseline. Publish results and decide on scale-up.

Success criteria agreed upfront — pilot costs borne by the project and CSR partners, not the civic body.

Beyond the pilot

Built for every scale of settlement

Place as configuration

Metro wards, town municipalities or village panchayats — localities, categories, language, currency and department mapping are all settings, not code.

Multilingual by design

The same platform speaks the local language of every deployment — built for India's languages first, ready for any country's.

Open civic data

Aggregated, anonymized issue data can support planning, budgeting and research — an asset that grows with every place that joins.

What's next

The road ahead

Native mobile app

iOS and Android apps as the main field client — fast reporting, offline tolerance, and push updates when an issue moves or an official responds.

Many Indian languages

Hindi, Kannada, Tamil, Telugu and more from the start — with voice input so low-literacy residents can report just by speaking.

Real "Show the fix" AI

On-device-quality AI vision that turns any photo and chosen solution into a realistic preview of the resolved spot.

Verified official directory

The full civic and elected hierarchy, synced from government sources and kept current as office-holders change.
